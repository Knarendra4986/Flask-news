Hurricane Melissa battered coastal communities, causing heavy rain and strong winds that damaged crops and fishing boats. Local aid agencies and international partners mobilized to provide emergency food, shelter, and medical assistance. Officials warned of continued flooding and urged residents to move to higher ground as storm surges increased.

Farmers reported widespread losses to staple crops, while fishers said many of their boats were overturned or damaged, disrupting livelihoods. The response effort emphasized the need for long-term support to rebuild infrastructure and restore economic activity for affected communities.
